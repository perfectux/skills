# Copy Checks

Use before final response when interface copy changed.

## Clarity

- The user can tell what the control, state, or message refers to.
- The copy uses product terms consistently.
- No implementation terms appear unless the audience is technical.
- The copy is short enough to scan in place.

## Action

- Primary actions use concrete verbs.
- Secondary actions are clearly lower commitment.
- Destructive actions name the object and consequence.
- Confirmation copy says whether the action can be undone.

## Recovery

- Errors explain what failed and what to do next.
- Empty states say what is missing and how to start.
- Permission copy says what access is needed and who can grant it.
- Loading copy does not imply fake precision or fake certainty.

## Trust

- No fake proof, fake metrics, vague guarantees, or unsupported claims.
- AI uncertainty, assumptions, missing inputs, or sources are visible when they affect decisions.
- Sensitive actions avoid casual or playful wording.

## Accessibility

- Visible labels and accessible names match.
- Status changes are clear when announced out of visual context.
- Link and button text make sense without surrounding paragraphs.
